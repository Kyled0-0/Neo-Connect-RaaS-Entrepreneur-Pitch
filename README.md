## What Is NeoConnect?

NeoConnect is a startup concept built around one problem: 53% of working-age Australians with disabilities are employed, compared to 84% of those without. The gap isn't about ability. It's structural. Inaccessible workplaces, physical presence requirements, and a lack of flexible hiring pipelines keep a large, skilled population out of the workforce entirely.

NeoConnect addresses this through a Robot-as-a-Service (RaaS) platform that combines VR hiring, immersive job training, and telepresence robotics. Workers with disabilities operate customer-facing robots remotely. Businesses get skilled staff without major workplace modifications. Everyone wins.

## The Problem

Australia has 5.5 million people with disabilities, 21.4% of the total population. Of the 2.7 million who are working age, only 48% are employed. For those with severe disabilities, that drops to 24%. Meanwhile, service industries like hospitality, retail, and healthcare are consistently short-staffed, and the roles they struggle to fill rely on communication and empathy more than physical presence.

The system isn't designed around these workers. NeoConnect is.

---

## The Solution

A three-part platform:

**Accessible hiring and training platform** — WCAG 2.2 AA compliant job portal with VR and video training modules tailored to specific roles and robot operation. Lowers the barrier to entry before day one.

**Telepresence robots** — HD cameras, microphones, job-specific attachments (serving trays, barista tools, displays), ultrasonic obstacle detection, and mapped safe zones. Supports eye-tracking, voice commands, joystick, and gesture control to accommodate varying physical abilities.

**VR integration** — Custom accessible VR headset with the option to switch to desktop or mobile. Gives remote workers a real sense of presence alongside on-site colleagues.

---

## Business Model

NeoConnect runs on a tiered RaaS subscription. Businesses pay a recurring fee covering robot access, platform features, and training. A dedicated share of that revenue goes directly to employee wages. The model is reinforced by government and NGO partnerships, seed investment, and value-add services like robot upgrades, analytics, and advanced VR training.

**Revenue streams:** Business subscriptions, government and NGO partnerships, seed/angel investment, value-add services.

## Growth Strategy

**Phase 1 (3-6 months):** Target hospitality, cafes, and retail facing immediate labour shortages. Launch RaaS pilots, secure government grants and NGO partnerships.

**Phase 2 (6-18 months):** Expand into education, corporate offices, and healthcare. Build case studies from pilot outcomes.

**Phase 3 (18+ months):** International expansion into Europe and North America. Integrate AI for semi-autonomous robot operation, opening new revenue streams.
